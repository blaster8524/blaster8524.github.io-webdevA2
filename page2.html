Why the term Soulslike?

The term Soulslike refers to games similar in terms of difficulty and aesthetics to Demon's Souls and its successor the Dark Souls series, in more general terms games that are inspired by the "Souls" series such as Demon's Souls developed by FromSoftware.

Other nicknames

Soulslike games developed by FromSoftware themselves are more specifically referred to as Soulsborne games, a portmanteau of Souls and Bloodborne. Soulslikes have been adopted by a number of critics and developers. However there is debate on whether Soulslike is a true genre, or rather just a pool of shared mechanics in games itself. Of course, the name Soulslike is just a cultural label, so people would generalise such games into the category, and would continue calling such games either Soulslike or Soulsborne if made by FromSoftware.

Gameplay of Soulslike Games

Soulslike games typically have a high level of difficulty where repeated player-character death is expected and incorporated as part of the gameplay, losing all progress if certain checkpoints have not been reached. Soulslike games also usually have means to permanently improve the player-character's abilities as to be able to progress further, often by a type of currency that can be earned and spent, but may be lost or abandoned between deaths if not appropriately managed, similar to the souls in the Souls series.

Setting and Plot

Soulslike games are commonly defined by their dark fantasy setting and lack of overt storytelling, as well as their deep worldbuilding, with a captivating world being cited as key to spark players' desire to explore. Players are meant to discover bits and pieces of the game's lore over time via environmental storytelling, item descriptions and cryptic dialogue, piecing it together themselves to increase the game's sense of mystery.
